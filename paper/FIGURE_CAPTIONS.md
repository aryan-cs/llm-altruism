# Figure Captions

Suggested manuscript-facing captions for the generated figures.

## Figure 1: Scarcity Society Final Survival

File:

- `paper/figures/society_reputation_live/society_reputation_final_survival.png`

Suggested caption:

> Final survival in the scarcity and public-reputation societies. In scarcity,
> `task-only` preserves `1.0000` final survival, while both `cooperative` and
> `competitive` fall to `0.8889`. Under public reputation, all completed
> prompt families preserve `1.0000` final survival.

## Figure 2: Society Trade Volume

File:

- `paper/figures/society_reputation_live/society_reputation_trade_volume.png`

Suggested caption:

> Trade volume in the scarcity and public-reputation societies. `Cooperative`
> prompting produces the most exchange in both worlds, but that added visible
> sociality does not translate into the best scarcity survival outcome.

## Figure 3: Society Alliance Count

File:

- `paper/figures/society_reputation_live/society_reputation_alliance_count.png`

Suggested caption:

> Alliance formation in the scarcity and public-reputation societies.
> `Cooperative` prompting induces visible alliance structure, whereas
> `task-only` and `competitive` remain alliance-free. Reputation preserves this
> organizational difference even after survival equalizes.

## Figure 4: Prisoner's Dilemma Neutral-Family Replication

File:

- `paper/figures/repaired_pd_replications/baseline_prompt_variants_cooperation.png`

Suggested caption:

> Precursor Prisoner's Dilemma baseline diagnostic pooled across two same-day
> cohorts. Literal `minimal-neutral` wording remains much less cooperative than
> the two more abstract neutral paraphrases, which collapse to the same action
> trace family on matched pooled trials.

## Figure 5: Cross-Game Precursor Baseline Comparison

File:

- `paper/figures/triplet_live/baseline_prompt_variants_cooperation.png`

Suggested caption:

> Cross-game precursor baseline comparison on the stable triplet cohort.
> Prisoner's Dilemma is much less cooperative than Chicken, while Stag Hunt is
> highest overall. These micro-level differences motivate why society claims
> should not be built on a single pairwise cooperation score.

## Figure 6: Benchmark Presentation Effects

File:

- `paper/figures/benchmark_live/benchmark_presentations_cooperation.png`

Suggested caption:

> Precursor benchmark-presentation comparison. In Prisoner's Dilemma,
> cooperation rises as the presentation becomes less canonical, while Chicken
> and Stag Hunt move in the opposite direction. Benchmark recognition is
> therefore real, but it is not a uniform cooperation-increasing effect.

## Figure 7: Prompt Susceptibility Across Core Games

File:

- `paper/figures/susceptibility_live/susceptibility_prompt_variants_cooperation.png`

Suggested caption:

> Precursor prompt-susceptibility comparison across the three core games.
> Cooperative prompting yields universal cooperation, but competitive prompting
> suppresses cooperation much more sharply in Prisoner's Dilemma than in
> Chicken or Stag Hunt.
