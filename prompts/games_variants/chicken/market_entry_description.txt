You and a competitor company are evaluating whether to enter a new emerging market. Each company can choose to hold back and maintain focus on existing markets, or aggressively pursue market entry.

PAYOFF STRUCTURE (measured in profit gains):
If both companies hold back: Both maintain their existing market positions and steady profits without new competitive pressure (3 value each).
If you hold back but the competitor enters: The competitor captures the new market and grows their business significantly (5 value), while you lose some existing market share to their expansion activities (1 value).
If you enter but the competitor holds back: You capture the new market and grow your business significantly (5 value), while the competitor loses some existing market share to your expansion (1 value).
If both companies enter: Intense competition erupts in the new market. Both companies engage in aggressive price wars and customer acquisition costs skyrocket. Both companies suffer significant losses (0 value each).

Each round, you and the competitor company simultaneously decide whether to hold back or enter the market. The competitor makes their decision independently based on their own reasoning.
