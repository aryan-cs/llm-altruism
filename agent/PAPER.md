# PAPER

## Purpose

This file is the publication roadmap.

It answers:

1. what the paper is trying to say
2. what it should not say
3. what evidence is central versus supporting

## Current Thesis

The paper is about whether LLM agents can sustain a society of their own.

More precisely:

- if a population of LLM agents must gather resources, trade, communicate,
  reproduce, and survive under scarcity, what kinds of societies emerge?
- which prompt families and institutions preserve collective survival?
- when visible sociality appears, is it actually society-serving?

## What Part 1 Is Doing

Part 1 is still valuable, but it is not the main study.

Use Part 1 to establish:

1. baseline instability under supposedly neutral wording
2. prompt steerability
3. benchmark-recognition effects
4. explicit-versus-indirect prompting effects, including fiction narratives
5. game-structure dependence

Do not use Part 1 as the paper's final takeaway.

## What The Paper Should Claim

The best claims now are:

1. Society viability is a distinct target from pairwise cooperation.
2. Prompt families that look more prosocial at the interaction level do not
   necessarily produce the best survival outcome at the society level.
3. Public reputation can stabilize survival without collapsing differences in
   trade, alliances, and broader social structure.
4. Repeated-game precursor probes are necessary because baseline policy,
   steerability, and benchmark recognition are all unstable enough to distort
   macro interpretation.

## What The Paper Should Not Claim

Avoid these framings:

- "LLMs are altruistic"
- "LLMs are selfish"
- "the Prisoner's Dilemma result is the main contribution"
- "cooperative prompts create better societies"
- "a single neutral prompt reveals the default policy"

## Strongest Evidence By Priority

### Priority 1: completed long-horizon ecology results

These are the headline results, but they are not complete yet.

- target experiment: the `24`-agent, `120`-round multi-resource ecology with
  explicit `food`, `water`, `energy`, and `health`
- required comparisons: `task-only`, `cooperative`, `competitive`, with and
  without public reputation
- current blocker: the live reruns on that canonical world are still in
  progress

### Priority 2: predecessor institutional results

- older scarcity bundle: `task-only` best final survival
- older reputation bundle: survival equalizes, but social structure does not

### Priority 3: precursor diagnostics that explain macro fragility

- PD neutral-family instability
- PD prompt steerability
- benchmark-presentation sensitivity
- cross-game ordering

### Priority 4: tooling and reproducibility

- prompt-stack logging
- audited summaries
- exact paired tests
- reproducible figure and PDF generation

## Recommended Paper Angle

The best angle is:

- repeated games are precursor probes
- the real object of study is whether LLM-only societies are self-sustaining
- institutions matter because they mediate the gap between visible sociality
  and collective resilience

That angle is stronger, more novel, and better matched to the corrected result
bundle than a Part 1-centered story.
